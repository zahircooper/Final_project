      Cardio

      Dogs have a high degree of stamina compared to humans.

      Wild wolves spend many days traveling through mountains and forests in search of food.

      Domestic dogs have retained many of these traits which enable them to sustain running and walking over long periods of time.

      A long body on four legs enables it to keep a steady rate of movement without exhausting its reserves of energy.

      A dog can also regulate its core temperature during intense exercise by panting and raising or lowering its fur.

      Core & Stretching

      When your dog has a strong core and in good shape, the entire body is better supported. This also means less stress on the joints and better balance when working or playing. There's less chance of injury when your dog builds a stronger core.

      Stretching is a great way to build better flexibility, mobility and help prevent future injuries.

      Common Exercises

      The following are common exercises that we encourage our clients to do as part of their daily exercise routine.

      Staircases
      The steps add an additional challenge to a dog's workout, as they engage different muscles than those used on. regular walk or run and add an extra level of difficulty with the change in elevation.
      WalkBacks
      Walking backwards takes a lot of focus and coordination for dogs. This maneuver improves rear leg strength and proprioception.
      WaterWalks
      Aquatic therapy an help to improve your pup's strength, range of motion and endurance. The buoyancy properties of water minimize weight bearing stress to the joints, which helps prevent tissue injuries, inflammation and pain.
